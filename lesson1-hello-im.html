Real Conversation — First Day

Read this conversation between Sara (teacher) and Adam (new student). Notice how Adam introduces himself. The gold words are the key phrases.

👨‍🎓

Hello! My name is Adam. I'm from Morocco.
👩‍🏫

👩‍🏫

🔎 Notice: Adam uses "I'm" — this is short for "I am". In spoken English, we almost always use the short form. You'll learn why in the next section!
Section 3 · Grammar in Context

The Verb To Be — Your First Tool

💡 You already used this verb in the dialogue! "I'm from Morocco" and "I'm 20 years old" — that am is the verb to be.

The Verb "To Be" — Full & Short Forms

I am = I'm
You are = You're
He / She / It is = He's / She's / It's
We / They are = We're / They're

How Adam introduces himself — broken down:

SentenceSubjectVerb to beInformation
I'm Adam.Iam (I'm)name
I'm from Morocco.Iam (I'm)country
I'm 20 years old.Iam (I'm)age
I'm a student.Iam (I'm)job/role
She's from Japan.Sheis (She's)country

❓ Making Questions

Statement: You are from Morocco. → Question: Are you from Morocco?
Statement: His name is Adam. → Question: What is his name? = What's his name?
➡ Flip the subject and verb to make a Yes/No question!

🎯 Tip: In "Nice to meet you", the word "to" is very short — it sounds like "tə" (unstressed). Native speakers say it fast: "Nice-tə-meet-you".
🌍 Arabic speakers: The "p" sound in English does not exist in Arabic (ب in Arabic is a "b"). Be careful: "please" not "blease". We will practice this more in Lesson 4!
